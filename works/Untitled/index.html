Created in response to a poem by Eduard Lukoyanov. The work combines a static documentary image of a shopping mall parking lot with a site-specific sound composition constructed from location recordings and a processed voice performance. The poem is delivered as a commercial announcement and integrated into the existing acoustic environment of the shopping centre, becoming almost indistinguishable from the informational and promotional messages that typically occupy such spaces. Rather than illustrating the text, the work relocates it. Political, historical and personal references embedded in the poem are displaced into the everyday landscape of consumption, where they begin to interact with the rhythms, sounds and visual structures of contemporary public space. The project explores the relationship between language, commerce and collective attention, examining how meaning changes when poetry is absorbed into the infrastructure of everyday life.
